Biography

Bhagat Singh (1907–1931) was one of the most influential revolutionaries of the Indian freedom struggle. Born on 28 September 1907 in Banga village, Punjab (now in Pakistan), he grew up in a patriotic family deeply involved in the fight against British rule. Inspired by freedom fighters like Kartar Singh Sarabha and movements like the Jallianwala Bagh massacre, Bhagat Singh dedicated his life to the cause of India’s independence. He was a brilliant student, a powerful writer, and a fearless leader. Along with his comrades, he founded revolutionary groups such as the Hindustan Socialist Republican Association (HSRA) to fight British oppression. He gained national fame when he and Batukeshwar Dutt threw bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly in 1929, shouting the slogan “Inquilab Zindabad” (“Long Live the Revolution”) to protest against unjust laws. Bhagat Singh was arrested and later tried for his role in the killing of British officer John Saunders. Despite being only 23 years old, he faced his execution with courage and dignity. On 23 March 1931, Bhagat Singh, along with Rajguru and Sukhdev, was hanged in Lahore Jail. Today, Bhagat Singh is remembered as Shaheed (Martyr) Bhagat Singh, a symbol of courage, sacrifice, and resistance against oppression. His ideas of equality, justice, and revolution continue to inspire generations.

Acheivements

  • 28 September 1907 – Born in Banga village, Punjab.
  • 1919 – At age 12, deeply moved by the Jallianwala Bagh massacre, decided to fight against British rule.
  • 1924 – Joined the Hindustan Republican Association (HRA), a revolutionary group.
  • 17 December 1928 – Along with Rajguru, killed John Saunders, a British police officer, to avenge the death of Lala Lajpat Rai.
  • 8 April 1929 – With Batukeshwar Dutt, threw bombs in the Central Legislative Assembly in Delhi, shouting “Inquilab Zindabad”.
  • 1929–1930 – Wrote several articles and essays in jail on revolution, socialism, and freedom.
  • 23 March 1931 – Executed in Lahore Jail along with Rajguru and Sukhdev, becoming a martyr at just 23 years of age.
